F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is this community about?

    This community is mainly about engaging folks from diverse technical backgrounds to come together and collaborate on different projects related to AI and Machine Learning.

  • What is the motivation behind this community?

    You can learn anything related to AI online for free. You can read research papers from top AI labs for free in arXiv. This is one of the best times to get started in AI research. But there's a caveat, good quality research always results from a group of people. It's hard to find a right partner for your research, especially when you're not from a research university. So our community helps you to find right colloborators for your research.

  • Can an absolute beginner join this community?

    Yes Absolutely. Anyone who is interested to learn about AI and engage in different types of projects are welcomed to this platform.

  • Can I get a partner for Kaggle competitions?

    Yes. Members are allowed to collaborate on different competitions. It is not only limited to Kaggle but other competitions too.

  • Can I get research opportunities from here?

    Yes. Our core members are currently engaged in different research projects that involves professors from different universities. So members get an opportunity to collaborate with them and hence stand a chance of securing national/international research opportunities.

  • Should I expect to get a study buddy here?

    Yes absolutely. Afterall this is a place where like minded people hang out. :)

  • What other opportunities can I get from this platform?

    The most important thing that you can get out of this community is Learning. You can also get to meet like-minded people, immerse yourself in doing high-quality research and publishing, applying for patents with our help, and also getting referred to top research companies.

  • Do I need to have a solid mathematical background to get started in AI research?

    It helps, but is not required when you're starting out. You can also learn the requisite math on the fly. It also depends on the research project that you're involved in, For example, NLP research is less math intensive than projects on Optimization algorithms that require a great deal of math. We also have a #math-for-ml slack channel, where you can ask doubts, and find a person to discuss all things math for DL.
